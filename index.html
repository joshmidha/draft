Healthcare as a Point of Sale System

August 31, 2025

Hospital-Centric Flow (Reordered System)

Patient → Hospital
Patient shows up, gives ID + insurance info. That’s it. No billing, no co-pays, no insurer interaction.

Hospital → Insurer
Hospital codes the visit, generates claim, and sends it directly to insurer.
Hospital handles:
– eligibility check
– pre-authorization (if required)
– claim submission
– adjudication
– appeal or resubmission if denied

Insurer → Hospital
Insurer pays the hospital directly after adjudication.
Settlement can be immediate or batch (daily/weekly).

Hospital → Patient (only if balance remains)
If deductible or coinsurance applies, hospital sends a simple post-visit bill. Patient pays hospital directly.

Core Change

Traditional order:
Patient → Hospital → Insurer → Patient (EOB) → Patient (Bill) → Patient ↔ Insurer (Appeals)

Redesigned order:
Patient → Hospital → Insurer → Hospital → Patient (if balance)

Difference

The patient no longer acts as intermediary. All insurance interaction is collapsed into a direct hospital–insurer channel. The patient only touches the system twice: at intake, and (if necessary) at final balance payment.

Benefits

  • Removes patient from insurance backend entirely
  • Faster hospital payments (like merchant settlements)
  • Appeals and disputes handled institutionally, not individually
  • Patients see one bill at most, instead of navigating claims + EOBs

On the Debt of the Democratic Republic of Congo

April 21, 2025

Not every war needs weapons. Some are fought with footnotes, contract clauses, and cobalt mines.

Context

The DRC has a $500 million Eurobond maturing in 2049. That’s small money on Wall Street — but for a state like the DRC, it’s a litigation magnet...

Step 3: Restructure the Narrative

Offer performance-linked instruments (cobalt warrants, GDP-linked notes)... Wrap with AfDB/IFC to make marketable.
